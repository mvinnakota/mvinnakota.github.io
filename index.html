About Me



I am a doctoral student at the Harris School of Public Policy at the University of Chicago. I use applied microeconomics and empirical methods to better understand how households, firms, and governments insure themselves against natural disaster risks.


I am a native New Jerseyan who currently calls Chicago my (second) home. When I'm not working on research, you can find me learning new dance styles, experimenting with recipes, or reading Margaret Atwood novels.
